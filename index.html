A New Era at the Helm

Cover Story

Sol Plaatje University Installs Prof Debra Meyer as Third Vice-Chancellor and Principal, and First Woman to Hold the Office

"An installation is not merely about the appointment of an individual. It is about the renewal of a collective covenant — between a university and its people; between knowledge and society; between leadership and responsibility." — Prof Debra Meyer, Vice-Chancellor and Principal

Sol Plaatje University (SPU) formally installed Professor Debra Meyer as its third Vice-Chancellor and Principal at a dignified academic ceremony held at the SPU Great Hall in Kimberley on Friday 22 May 2026 — a historic moment as Professor Meyer becomes the first woman to hold this office in the University's history.

The installation was attended by dignitaries from across the South African higher education sector, national and provincial government, the judiciary, business, and the University community, anchored in SPU's identity as an academic institution shaped by the intellectual legacy of Solomon Tshekisho Plaatje.

Five Strategic Priorities for the Future

  1. 1. Expanding the University's research footprint
  2. 2. Advancing true multilingualism
  3. 3. Integration of modern technology, particularly artificial intelligence
  4. 4. Transitioning to an entrepreneurial university
  5. 5. Defining and strengthening the University's role as an anchor institution

"May future generations look back on this period and say that we chose contribution rather than comfort; we chose meaning not mediocrity; we chose possibility not limitation."

Chancellor

Thebe Ikalafeng Inaugurated as Second Chancellor of SPU

Held in the 150th year of Solomon Plaatje's birth, the inauguration placed the life and legacy of Plaatje at the centre of institutional expression. The Chancellor's gown was designed by Kimberley-born Thebe Magugu — Africa's first LVMH Prize winner.

"The future of the region lies not in the extraction of physical resources but in the cultivation of knowledge, talent and intellectual capacity." — Chancellor Thebe Ikalafeng

Vice-Chancellor Prof Debra Meyer has been featured in a public mural in Eldorado Park — a community-led initiative recognising individuals who contribute meaningfully to society. A partnership between community broadcaster EldosFM and the Go Green Go Clean project, with artwork by artist Charlton Brown.

Historic Partnership

Student Debt Relief Initiative with South32 Brings Hope

A partnership between South32 and Sol Plaatje University is addressing one of the most pressing challenges facing students today. Keynote speaker Mrs Tumelo Dilotsohle encouraged beneficiaries to leave with pride and confidence and to become a source of light within their communities.

The programme focuses on education and leadership, economic participation, good health and social wellbeing, and the responsible use of natural resources. Over the past two years, the initiative has funded a significant number of learners alongside investment in schools and in mathematics and English.

Prof Jose Frantz, DVC: Research, Innovation and Postgraduate Studies, reminded students that behind every debt is a personal story and that education restores hope.

03 — Research & Academic Leadership

Scholarship & Leadership Growing Stronger

Research Milestone

SPU Secures Its First SARChI Research Chair

Sol Plaatje University has reached a significant research milestone with the appointment of Professor Boitumelo Diale as the University's first holder of a South African Research Chairs Initiative (SARChI) Chair, in Inclusive Rural Education and Early Childhood Development.

Prof Diale is an NRF C2-rated researcher and registered Educational Psychologist with a distinguished record spanning senior roles at the University of Johannesburg and a visiting professorship at the University of Nigeria Nsukka.

"This Chair is not about an individual's achievement — it is about building a collective research endeavour that serves children, educators, and communities."

— Prof Boitumelo Diale
Academic Leadership

Prof Dipane Hlalele Appointed Dean of the Faculty of Education

Appointed for a five-year term from 1 April 2026, Prof Hlalele is an NRF C2-rated researcher who has supervised more than 60 postgraduate students to completion, including 38 doctoral candidates.

Finance Leadership

SPU CFO Appointed to SAICA National Council

Ms Annalene Marais, SPU's Chief Financial Officer, has been appointed to the National Council of the South African Institute of Chartered Accountants — contributing sector insight to national engagements on governance and ethical leadership.

Communications Leadership

Ms Mosima Mehlape Appointed to MACE Board of Directors

The Manager: Communication and Marketing at SPU has been appointed to the Board of Directors of MACE — the first time in SPU's history that a staff member has been selected to serve on the MACE Board. Her work contributed to SPU's recognition among South Africa's Top Ten Coolest Universities at the 2023 GenNXT Awards.

SPU Accounting Programme Receives Professional Endorsement

The Bachelor of Commerce in Accounting has been professionally endorsed by both SAIPA and SAICA, broadening professional pathways for graduates and supporting employability within the accounting profession.

"This recognition reflects the Faculty's deliberate focus on academic quality, professional relevance and curriculum alignment," said Prof Pierre Joubert, Dean of the Faculty of Economic and Management Sciences.

South African Reserve Bank Engages SPU Students

The SARB's Economic Research Department visited SPU, covering the Bank's mandate in maintaining price stability, safeguarding financial stability and contributing to sustainable economic growth — bridging academic learning with real-world application.

Future Accounting Professionals Engage Industry Through Business Simulation

Over 100 BCom Accounting students navigated the Commercium business simulation on 25 April 2026, developing strategic thinking, collaboration and problem-solving skills alongside representatives of the Auditor-General of South Africa.

Marking International Day of Mathematics

Students and lecturers marked Pi Day on 13 March with interactive activities. A highlight was a robotics demonstration by Mrs Eva Mamabolo and her team — recent winners of a major technology competition in Gauteng — showing how mathematical principles underpin robotics programming.

Mr "Mwalimu" Mlamuli Tabhu has been awarded R275 480 by the NRF under the Black Academics Advancement Programme for his project on Decolonising the Episteme of African History — repositioning African perspectives at the centre of historical inquiry.

Student Support

SPU Hosts Sharp Calculator Skills Workshop

More than 100 third-year Mathematics students attended a workshop on 31 March 2026 to strengthen preparedness for assessments in trigonometry. All participating students received a Sharp scientific calculator.

Public Lecture

When Universities Are Captured

The Faculty of Humanities hosted a public lecture by Professor Jonathan Jansen of Stellenbosch University on 4 May 2026, under the theme Why South African Universities Fail: Reflections on Institutional Capture and Its Aftermaths.

The lecture drew strong participation in person and online, with attendees joining from across South Africa and internationally — including Africa, Europe, the United States and Canada — alongside officials from the Department of Higher Education and Training.

07 — Sport

A Season of Character

A Season of Transition, Character and Silver Linings

The 2026 Varsity Shield season was a defining chapter for SPU Rugby — marked by transition, resilience and the emergence of new talent. Following the departure of 12 senior players, 12 young players fresh from high school were introduced to the demands of university-level rugby.

Week after week, a young side competed with determination against more experienced opponents, gaining invaluable exposure to the pace, intensity and physical demands of Varsity Shield rugby.

Silver Linings

  • Jan Potgieter earned his 50th cap for SPU Rugby in the final match.
  • Khanya Kama selected for the Team South Africa FISU 2026 training squad.
  • Theo Ngqwala selected as Team Manager for Team South Africa FISU 2026.
  • Mandolene Norman, Strength and Conditioning Coach, selected for the Griquas Ladies U20 squad.
